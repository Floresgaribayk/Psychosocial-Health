Peer-Reviewed Articles

Compassion and Mental Health: A Neurobiological Perspective

This study examines how compassion affects mental health from a neurobiological standpoint. Findings suggest that higher levels of compassion are linked to improved emotional regulation and resilience.

Journal: Nature

Link: Read Article

The Impact of Compassion on Physiological Health

Investigates how compassion influences physiological health markers such as blood pressure and stress response. Results support that compassion has measurable benefits for overall physical health.

Journal: Springer

Link: Read Article

Compassion and Stress Recovery Mechanisms

Explores mechanisms by which compassion helps in stress recovery. Demonstrates that compassion practice accelerates recovery from physiological stress.

Scientifically Based Websites

Greater Good Science Center – UC Berkeley

Compassion releases natural chemicals in the brain that reduce stress and improve mood. These effects support heart health, lower blood pressure, and promote emotional balance. Simple daily acts of kindness improve relationships and physical/mental well-being.

Website: Visit Website

BMC Medical Education – Compassion in Healthcare Review

This review studied compassion in healthcare between 2015–2020. Results show compassion improves patient care, increases satisfaction, and strengthens emotional well-being. Only a few studies tested training programs, showing a need for more structured compassion education.
