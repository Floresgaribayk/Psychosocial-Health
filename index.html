What is Compassion? According to Stephen Trzeciak in the text “Compassionomics”, Compassion is the emotional response to another’s pain or suffering, involving an authentic desire to help. (Trzeciak, 2019)

What is Physiology? Physiology is the study of the mechanism of living things. Research in this field aims to build an understanding of how the body operates when in health and when in disease. (What is physiology? 2025)

This website intends to highlight the important link between acts of compassion and positive physiological responses.

Peer-Reviewed Articles

Compassion and Mental Health (Nature)

Behaviors of compassion have been associated with improved levels of happiness and mental well-being, decreased inflammation, and lower risk of cardiovascular events. These physiological health benefits are also associated with reduced levels of loneliness that are mediated by compassion. Behaviors of compassion include: Compassion toward self (CTS) and Compassion toward others (CTO). Higher levels of CTO and CTS were found to be predictors of lower levels of loneliness. Lower levels of loneliness is correlated with improved health outcomes. On the other hand, high levels of loneliness are associated with higher levels of mortality. Performing behaviors of compassion towards oneself and others improves health outcomes through the reduction of loneliness.
