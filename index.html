About

Compassion is a vital and powerful tool that not promotes a positive emotional response; but has been scientifically proven to benefit human physiology. Growing research has shown that the act of giving and receiving compassion is an excellent tool in calming physiological responses to stress and reducing patient mortality rates.

Scientifically Based Websites

Greater Good Science Center – UC Berkeley

Compassion and kindness do make the world a nicer place. It helps us feel happier and healthier. Research shows that showing or receiving kindness releases natural chemicals that boost mood and calm stress. Being compassionate can improve heart health, lower blood pressure, and support emotional balance. Simple acts like checking in on a friend, being patient, or showing gratitude can strengthen relationships and foster a healthier mind.

BMC Medical Education – Compassion in Healthcare Review

This review explored how compassion is studied and practiced in healthcare between 2015 and 2020. Researchers examined what compassion means for patients and healthcare workers. Compassion is linked to better care, greater patient satisfaction, and improved emotional well-being. Some studies tested ways to teach or strengthen compassion in clinical settings, highlighting the need for more structured programs and research.
